# Demo Feedback Plan

## Purpose

The RiverLaunch.app demo should be used to test whether the community-driven River Wye concept makes sense to real paddlers before we build backend systems.

The goal is not to validate every map detail. The goal is to learn whether the workflow, data categories, and trust model feel useful.

## Audience

Show the demo to:

- River Wye canoeists
- open canoe paddlers
- club trip leaders
- coaches
- beginners who have planned a river trip recently
- people who maintain local paddling knowledge informally

## What to Test

## Section Planning

Questions:

- Can users understand the selected river section quickly?
- Do put-in, take-out, distance, level, access, hazards, and reports appear in the right order?
- Is the map useful alongside the section detail panel?
- Are section names and splits understandable?

## Community Contributions

Questions:

- Would users add a hazard, report, photo, or feature?
- Does map-click placement feel natural?
- Is "date observed" more useful than "date posted"?
- Should reports ask for craft type?
- Are contribution categories clear?
- What would make contributing feel too much effort?

## Trust and Freshness

Questions:

- Do users understand confirmed, stale, disputed, and resolved states?
- What makes a hazard report trustworthy?
- Is a photo important for hazard credibility?
- How old is too old for different report types?
- Who should be allowed to resolve a hazard?

## Access Notes

Questions:

- Are access notes helpful?
- Does the wording avoid sounding like legal advice?
- What access details matter most?
- Should access notes be reviewed before appearing publicly?

## Gauge Interpretation

Questions:

- Does the gauge card provide enough context?
- Do users need more than one gauge?
- How should "good", "low", and "high" be presented?
- Should runnable ranges be shown only after local validation?

## Suggested Demo Script

1. Open the Wye section list.
2. Select Hoarwithy to Ross-on-Wye.
3. Ask the user what they understand from the section page.
4. Ask what information is missing before they would travel.
5. Click the map to place a hazard or feature.
6. Add observed date and craft type.
7. Confirm or resolve an existing hazard.
8. Ask whether they would do this after a real trip.

## Feedback Capture

Record:

- participant type
- paddling experience
- rivers they know
- sections they would expect in the Wye pilot
- missing data categories
- confusing labels
- trust concerns
- contribution friction
- willingness to contribute
- willingness to review/maintain a section

## Success Signals

Strong signals:

- users ask to add real local data
- users understand why freshness matters
- users can explain the app's value back in their own words
- club/trip leader users see maintenance value
- beginners understand what they still need to check

Weak signals:

- users see it as "just another map"
- users do not trust community reports
- users think contributing is too slow
- users cannot distinguish official data from community guidance
- access notes feel too vague or too legally definitive

## Output

After five to ten feedback sessions, update:

- River Wye seed dataset
- feature taxonomy
- community model
- MVP spec
- demo UI priorities
