# Product Brief

## Working Concept

River Go is a UK-first canoeing app that combines live river data with community-maintained local knowledge. The app helps paddlers understand river sections before they travel: current levels, access points, hazards, photos, portages, runnable ranges, and recent condition reports.

The product is not just a gauge map. Its core value is a trusted, structured, and fresh knowledge layer built by paddlers.

## Positioning

The UK river guide for canoeists: live levels, access clarity, hazards, photos, and local river knowledge in one place.

## Initial Market

Start with the UK, then expand into Europe once the contribution model, river-section model, and data-ingestion patterns are proven.

The UK is a strong starting market because:

- River access and licensing are confusing and vary by waterway.
- Canoeists rely on fragmented information from clubs, guidebooks, forums, apps, and word of mouth.
- Current river-level tools are useful but do not fully solve the section-level planning problem.
- Community freshness matters because hazards, trees, access, and portages can change quickly.

## Target Users

Primary users:

- Recreational canoeists planning river trips.
- Open canoe paddlers looking for suitable sections.
- Club paddlers sharing local knowledge.
- Beginners trying to understand where they can paddle safely and legally.

Secondary users:

- Whitewater-adjacent paddlers.
- Kayakers and SUP paddlers where the route data overlaps.
- Coaches, clubs, and trip leaders.

## Core Jobs

### Can I paddle this river section soon?

Show current level, trend, relevant gauge data, recent reports, known hazards, and community guidance for the section.

### What is this section like?

Show put-in, take-out, distance, character, suitability, portages, access notes, parking, photos, and major features.

### What has changed recently?

Highlight new hazards, fallen trees, blocked access, high/low water reports, temporary closures, and resolved issues.

### Can I contribute useful local knowledge?

Make it easy to add photos, hazards, features, access notes, condition reports, and confirmations.

## Product Principles

- River sections are the primary unit of planning.
- Community contributions should be typed, structured, date-stamped, and verifiable.
- Hazards and access notes need freshness and confidence indicators.
- The app should avoid claiming that a river is "safe"; it should present recent information and clear uncertainty.
- Canoe-specific usefulness matters more than generic outdoor mapping breadth.
- The first version should be useful without requiring full trip tracking, offline maps, or social networking.

## Differentiation

River Go should differentiate on:

- UK-first access and river-section context.
- Canoe-specific suitability and trip planning.
- Community-verified hazards and features.
- Photos attached to real river objects.
- Freshness indicators for changing conditions.
- Runnable-level guidance tied to specific sections, not just raw gauge readings.

## Initial Success Criteria

Early product success should be measured by whether users can answer:

- Where can I get in and out?
- What is the current level doing?
- Is this section likely to be suitable for my canoeing ability and craft?
- What are the known hazards?
- Has anyone paddled or checked it recently?
- What has changed since the last known report?
