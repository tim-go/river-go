# River Go: No Advice, No Go/No-Go

This is a hard product and legal invariant. Read it before writing any user-facing
copy, status indicator, badge, or feature that interprets river conditions.

Owning spec: `/docs/specs/principles/no-advice-and-liability-language.md`.

## The Rule

River Go presents **facts**, never a safety verdict. It must never tell a user
whether it is safe to paddle, or imply a go/no-go decision for them.

- Present grade, gauge readings, community reports, source, and freshness as
  **context**, with visible uncertainty — not as a recommendation.
- Never use "safe to paddle", "safe for your experience", "approved route", or
  "official route" (unless a licensed source genuinely owns that route), and
  never imply that a tick/cross alone decides whether someone should paddle.
- Prefer `runnable`, `good range`, `high`, `no recent reading`, `unknown` over
  `safe`. Prefer `river`, `section`, `known feature`, `community report`,
  `recent level context`, `source confidence`.

When in doubt, show the data and its source and let the paddler decide.
